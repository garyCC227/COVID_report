    (Updated: 30 Jan 2020 09:05AM)

    TOKYO: Three Japanese citizens among more than 200 evacuated from China have tested positive for the new coronavirus, Japan's health minister said Thursday (Jan 30).

    The three people arrived in Japan on Wednesday, on the first flight to evacuate Japanese citizens from the Chinese city of Wuhan, the epicentre of a deadly outbreak that has killed 170 people and infected thousands.

    Two of the three had not shown any symptoms, the ministry said, according to NHK.
    READ: Wuhan virus death toll rises to 170, more than 1,700 new cases

    The three evacuees raise the number of cases in Japan so far to 11, including two people who appear to have contracted the virus without travelling to China.

    "In addition to the eight cases, among the people who returned from Wuhan yesterday, infection (with the new coronavirus) has been confirmed in one person with symptoms and two other people who have no symptoms," Health Minister Katsunobu Kato told parliament...

                              Number of confirmed cases in Japan rises to 33
                              Posted: 05/02/2020-13:29

                              Author:
                              Flynn

                              According to news from Tokyo, as of 12 am local time on Wednesday, data show that a total of 33 cases of pneumonia diagnosed with new coronavirus infection in Japan have been reached. Japan's Cabinet Chief Cabinet Secretary Yoshihide also said that the Japanese side does not intend to postpone Chinese President Xi Jinping at this stage. It is expected to visit Japan in April and hopes to make the necessary preparations as planned.

                              It is reported that Japan confirmed three new cases on Tuesday: a Japanese woman who returned to China by charter flight from Wuhan on January 30, a woman living in Wuhan, and a man living in Hubei Province. All three tested negative for the initial virus and confirmed infection in subsequent tests. Japanese women who returned to China by charter flight had fever and cough after returning to China. They were tested for the virus immediately, but no infection was confirmed. It was tested again on February 4 and confirmed to be infected.

                              The woman, who lives in Wuhan, arrived in Japan on January 21, was hospitalized on the 31st, and was tested for a virus. No infection was detected. But on February 3, her condition did not improve, and she was tested for the virus again. On the 4th, she was infected. Another man who lived in Hubei Province arrived in Japan on January 22 with related symptoms. He was not confirmed by the test on the 26th and returned to China on the 30th. However, the Japanese National Institute of Infectious Diseases tested his test materials again on February 3 and confirmed the infection on the 4th.

                              In response to Xi Jinping's plan for a later visit to Japan, Kyodo News previously quoted diplomatic sources reporting that due to the expansion of the new coronavirus epidemic, the two governments were scheduled to hold a working-level preparation meeting in Beijing this month and postponed the consultation on marine affairs. It has been speculated that Xi Jinping's state visit may also be delayed. China's Ministry of Foreign Affairs has previously pointed out on related issues that China and Japan should continue to work together on Xi Jinping's visit to Japan.

                              In terms of the epidemic situation in Japan, on the morning of February 5th, the cruise ship "Diamond Princess" docked in the offshore Yokohama was undergoing large-scale quarantine. News was first sent to those who had fever, cough and other symptoms and had close contact with confirmed cases A total of 273 people were sampled and tested. Of these, 31 patients have given results and 10 have been diagnosed without critically ill patients. The remaining 3,700 passengers and staff remained in principle for 14 days inside the ship.
